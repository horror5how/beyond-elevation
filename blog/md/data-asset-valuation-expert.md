# How to Hire a Data Asset Valuation Expert (And Why Most Founders Hire the Wrong One)

74% of first-time data valuations undercount the asset by 3-5x. The reason is straightforward: founders hire accountants to do operator work. An accountant values what has been purchased. Your data was built, enriched, and compounded over years of operations. That is a fundamentally different valuation problem — and it demands a fundamentally different data asset valuation expert.

Hayat Amin, who has priced data assets across three exits and structured the DGS data monetisation deal that most consultants said was impossible, argues the expert you hire determines whether your data shows up as a rounding error or a seven-figure line item. The gap between those outcomes is not the data. It is who values it.

## What Does a Data Asset Valuation Expert Actually Do?

A data asset valuation expert quantifies the economic value of proprietary datasets, data pipelines, and data-derived products using methods designed specifically for internally generated, non-purchased data assets. Unlike a traditional appraiser who pulls comparable transactions, a data valuation expert models future revenue potential, exclusivity premiums, and replacement cost — because most proprietary datasets have no market comparable.

The work spans three dimensions. First, the data audit: cataloguing what data exists, its quality, refresh rate, and uniqueness. Second, revenue modelling: projecting licensing revenue, cost savings, or the valuation premium the data enables. Third, defensibility scoring: assessing how difficult the dataset is to replicate, how legally protected it is, and what moat it creates.

This is not accounting. This is [data monetisation strategy](/blog/posts/data-monetization-strategy-framework/) applied at the asset level. Beyond Elevation runs this process as part of every data engagement, and the output feeds directly into fundraising decks, M&A data rooms, and licensing negotiations.

## Why Most Founders Hire the Wrong Data Asset Valuation Expert

The most common mistake is hiring a Big Four accounting firm to run your enterprise data valuation. Their teams are credentialed in purchased-asset valuation — goodwill, trademarks, customer lists acquired in M&A. They apply the same purchased-intangible frameworks to your internally built data. The result: a valuation that reflects replacement cost at best, and zero at worst, because IFRS and GAAP do not allow internally generated intangible assets on the balance sheet without a specific structuring vehicle.

The second mistake is hiring a pure-play data scientist. Data scientists understand quality, volume, and statistical significance. They do not understand how investors price defensibility, how licensees negotiate royalty rates, or how M&A buyers weight data in their bid models. A data asset appraisal that cannot speak the language of capital is a document that sits in a drawer.

Hayat Amin calls this the "data valuation gap" — the distance between what your data is actually worth in a transaction and what a generalist valuer puts on paper. In one engagement, a 12-person team's dataset was initially appraised at £400K by a Big Four firm. After a proper valuation using the income approach and licensing-revenue modelling, the asset was revalued at £2.3M — and that number held in the [data licensing deal](/blog/posts/data-licensing-explained/) that followed.

## Hayat Amin's Data Valuation Expert Scorecard — The 5-Point Checklist

Hayat Amin's Data Valuation Expert Scorecard is the diagnostic Beyond Elevation runs before recommending any data valuation provider to a client. Five criteria separate the experts who produce defensible numbers from those who produce expensive guesswork.

**1. Transaction experience.** Has the expert's valuation survived an actual deal — licensing, M&A, or fundraise? A dataset worth calculation that has never been tested in a negotiation is an academic exercise.

**2. Methodology range.** Can they run income approach, market approach, and cost approach? Data assets routinely require blended methods because no single approach captures the full picture.

**3. Licensing fluency.** Does the expert understand royalty rate structures, exclusivity tiers, and field-of-use restrictions? Data licensing is the primary monetisation route for most datasets. A valuer who cannot model [licensing revenue](/blog/posts/know-how-licensing-hidden-revenue/) will undercount the asset.

**4. Legal-structural knowledge.** Can they advise on structuring vehicles — like the Isle of Man Data Asset Foundation — that allow [data to sit on the balance sheet](/blog/posts/data-asset-on-balance-sheet/)? Without this, the valuation produces a number that cannot flow into the financials.

**5. Investor translation.** Can the expert present the valuation in a format VCs and acquirers trust? Scenario-weighted outputs with clear assumptions, not a single point estimate buried in a 90-page PDF.

If your prospective data asset valuation expert fails two or more of these criteria, they will produce a number. But the number will not survive its first contact with a sophisticated buyer.

## How Does Data Asset Valuation Change Your Fundraising Math?

A properly valued data asset shifts the fundraising conversation from revenue multiples to asset-backed defensibility. Companies with documented, valued IP and data assets are 10.2x more likely to secure early-stage funding. At Series B and beyond, a data asset that has been independently valued and structured onto the balance sheet commands a 15-25% valuation premium over comparable companies without one.

Hayat Amin reminds founders that the valuation is not the asset — the negotiating leverage it creates is the asset. When a VC sees a data asset independently valued at £3M on a structured balance sheet, three things happen: due diligence shifts from "do you have defensibility?" to "how deep is the defensibility?"; the term sheet reflects a higher pre-money; and founder dilution drops.

Beyond Elevation's [data valuation](/blog/posts/ai-training-data-valuation/) clients routinely use their data asset valuation as the opening move in fundraising — not an afterthought buried in the appendix of a pitch deck.

## When Should You Bring In a Data Asset Valuation Expert?

Hire a data asset valuation expert 90 days before the event that puts a price on your company. That means 90 days before a fundraise, 90 days before an M&A process, 90 days before a licensing negotiation, or 90 days before a board meeting where valuation is on the agenda.

Hiring earlier is fine but rarely necessary. Hiring later — which is what most founders do — means the valuation is rushed, the methodology is shallow, and the number arrives after the negotiation has already anchored on a lower figure.

Three trigger events should prompt an immediate enterprise data valuation even outside a transaction window:

- You discover a competitor is licensing similar data at rates that imply your dataset is worth multiples of what you assumed
- A potential acquirer or partner requests your data room and you have no data asset documentation
- You are structuring an IP holding company and need to determine which data assets transfer and at what value

Hayat Amin's rule on timing: "If you are about to put a price on your company, you should already know the price of your data. If you do not, someone else will price it for you — and they will price it low."

## FAQ

### How much does a data asset valuation cost?

A credible data asset valuation from a specialist typically costs £15K-£50K depending on the complexity of the data landscape, the number of datasets, and whether the valuation needs to be audit-ready. Big Four firms charge 2-3x more for equivalent scope. The ROI threshold is simple: if the valuation increases your negotiating position by more than 10x its cost, it pays for itself.

### Can I value my data assets without hiring an expert?

You can produce an internal estimate using the income approach — projected licensing revenue multiplied by probability-weighted scenarios. But self-assessed valuations carry no weight in fundraising, M&A, or litigation. An independent third-party valuation is required for the number to hold credibility with investors, acquirers, or regulators.

### What is the difference between data valuation and data monetisation?

Data valuation determines what your data is worth. [Data monetisation](/blog/posts/data-monetization-strategy-framework/) turns that value into revenue through licensing, partnerships, or product integration. Valuation is the diagnostic; monetisation is the treatment. The best outcomes happen when the same team handles both, because the valuation methodology directly informs the monetisation strategy.

### Is data asset valuation different from IP valuation?

Data asset valuation is a subset of IP valuation, but it requires specialised methods. Traditional IP valuation focuses on patents, trademarks, and copyrights — assets with clear legal boundaries and established comparable transaction databases. Data assets lack standardised legal frameworks in most jurisdictions, have no universal registry, and their value depends heavily on context: who uses the data, for what purpose, and what alternatives exist. A generalist IP valuer undervalues data assets unless they have specific experience in data licensing and data-as-a-service models.
