The legal answer is 20 years. The answer that matters to your licensing program — and your exit multiple — is closer to 7. Here is why most patents expire before they should, and the three dates every founder needs to know.

How long does a patent last? A US utility patent lasts 20 years from the earliest effective non-provisional filing date. Design patents last 15 years from the date of grant. But three additional expiration mechanisms — one administrative, one commercial, one regulatory — determine whether your patent reaches its term and what it is worth when it gets there. Most founders only know one of the three.

## How Long Does a Patent Last? The Statutory Answer

The 20-year utility patent term is measured from the date of the earliest non-provisional application in the patent family — not from the date of grant. Because patent prosecution typically takes 2–4 years, a patent granted today from a 2022 filing has roughly 16 years of enforceable life remaining, not 20.

Design patents last 15 years from the date of grant (changed from 14 to 15 in December 2023 to align with the Hague Agreement). Plant patents last 20 years from the filing date. Provisional patent applications last exactly 12 months — not 20 years — and must be converted to a utility application within that window or the priority date is permanently lost.

## The 3 Patent Expiration Dates That Determine a Patent's Real Lifespan

**Expiration Date 1: Maintenance fee cliffs.** US utility patents require maintenance fees at 3.5 years, 7.5 years, and 11.5 years after the date of grant. Fees run from $2,000 to $8,000 per payment for large entities. Miss a payment and the patent lapses. The USPTO provides a 6-month grace period with a late surcharge, but after that window closes, the patent is abandoned permanently and cannot be reinstated. European national-phase patents require annual renewal fees starting as early as year 3, adding another layer for international portfolios.

**Expiration Date 2: The commercial useful life.** Most technology patents are commercially relevant for 5–8 years from filing, not 20. A 2026 filing on a novel AI data pipeline may be largely designed around or technologically superseded by 2033. This is the expiration date that matters most for patent licensing revenue: the window during which competitors have sufficient economic incentive to pay a royalty rather than wait for expiration. Beyond Elevation's IP valuation framework starts with commercially useful life — not statutory term — because this is where licensing revenue is concentrated.

**Expiration Date 3: Patent term adjustments and extensions.** Patent term adjustment (PTA) compensates for USPTO prosecution delays — those extra days are added to your term. The average PTA for tech patents runs 400–800 days. Patent term extension (PTE) applies exclusively to FDA-regulated products and can add up to 5 years. Checking PTA entitlement is a zero-cost way to add 1–2 years of commercial life to recently granted patents.

## How Long Does a Patent Last Internationally?

International patent duration aligns with the 20-year utility term under TRIPS in most major jurisdictions.

- **Europe**: 20-year term from filing, but annual national renewal fees required in each designated country. Missing one fee permanently abandons that national patent.
- **China**: 20 years for invention patents; utility model patents limited to 10 years.
- **UK**: 20-year utility term with annual renewal fees from year 5, administered by UKIPO (~£700–£1,600 per year).

## What Happens When a Patent Expires?

When a patent expires, the technology enters the public domain. Anyone can use, manufacture, or sell products based on the formerly protected invention without a licence or royalty payment.

The final 2–3 years before expiration are when licensing negotiations are most time-sensitive. The licensee's incentive to settle increases as remaining royalty exposure shrinks relative to litigation costs.

When Beyond Elevation restructured Position Imaging's 66-patent portfolio, expiration mapping was one of the first analyses — identifying which patents were in the first half of their commercial life versus approaching the end of strategic relevance. The eight-figure licensing program that followed was built on this timeline intelligence.

## How to Extend the Effective Life of Your Patents

File continuations while the parent patent is still pending. Continuations inherit the parent's priority date while introducing new claims covering current competitor implementations. A well-structured patent family of continuations and divisionals can deliver 10–15 years of commercially relevant, evolving claim coverage from a single original filing date.

Price licences based on commercially useful life, not remaining statutory term. Licensing negotiations initiated 3–5 years before commercial obsolescence are structurally stronger than those begun in the final 12 months of term.

## FAQ

### How long does a utility patent last?

A US utility patent lasts 20 years from the earliest effective non-provisional filing date. Because patent prosecution takes 2–4 years, most granted utility patents have 16–18 years of enforceable life remaining at grant. Maintenance fees must be paid at 3.5, 7.5, and 11.5 years after grant to keep the patent in force through its full term.

### How long does a design patent last?

A US design patent lasts 15 years from the date of grant. No maintenance fees are required for US design patents. Design patents filed before December 2023 last 14 years from grant under the prior rule.

### Can a patent be renewed after 20 years?

No — US utility patents cannot be renewed beyond the 20-year statutory term. The two exceptions are patent term adjustment (PTA), which compensates for USPTO prosecution delays, and patent term extension (PTE), which applies only to FDA-regulated products and can add up to 5 years.

### What happens if I miss a patent maintenance fee?

If a maintenance fee payment is missed and the 6-month grace period also passes, the patent lapses permanently and cannot be reinstated. Diarising maintenance fee deadlines from the day of grant is the most effective safeguard against accidental lapse.

### How long does a provisional patent last?

A provisional patent application lasts exactly 12 months from its filing date. If no non-provisional utility application is filed within that window, the provisional expires and the priority date is permanently lost. Provisional applications cannot be renewed or extended.
