Intangible asset valuation is the process of assigning a defensible dollar figure to non-physical assets — patents, proprietary datasets, trade secrets, software, brand equity, and documented know-how. ## How Do You Actually Value Intangible Assets?

Three methods. Each serves a different purpose. The best valuations use all three and triangulate.

**Income approach.** Project the future cash flows attributable to the intangible asset. Discount them back to present value. This works best for patents and data assets with clear revenue linkage. If your patent portfolio protects $8 million in annual revenue and a reasonable royalty rate is 6%, that portfolio is generating $480,000 per year in attributable value — and its present value over the remaining patent life is substantial.

**Market approach.** Find comparable transactions — what have similar patents, datasets, or trade secret portfolios sold or licensed for? Ktmine, ROL Solutions, and public SEC filings provide benchmarks. This method is the most persuasive to investors because it uses real market evidence, not projections.

**Cost approach.** Calculate what it would cost to recreate the asset from scratch. This sets the floor value. If your dataset took 14 engineers 2.5 years to build and curate, the replacement cost is north of $5 million before you factor in opportunity cost and the learning curve a competitor would face.
