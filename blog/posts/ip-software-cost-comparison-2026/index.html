The Pattern in the Pricing

Notice what you are paying for as you climb the table: data curation and workflow, not answers. A $100K stack and a free stack both end at the same place, a human looking at search results deciding what they mean for filings, licensing, and valuation. The software market charges for everything except that last step, which is the only step that makes money.

FAQ

What is the cheapest way to do patent analytics in 2026?

Lens.org plus Google Patents, both free, cover search, families, and basic landscapes. The first paid tier worth considering is PatBase for professional search volume.

How much should a startup spend on IP software?

Under 50 patents: close to zero on software. Spend on filings and on strategy. The expensive platforms earn their keep only at portfolio sizes and search volumes most startups never reach.

Which IP platform has the best value for money?

For pure search volume, PatBase. For dashboards and ease, PatSnap at a hard-negotiated price. For most companies under 100 patents, the honest answer is free databases plus paid human analysis.
