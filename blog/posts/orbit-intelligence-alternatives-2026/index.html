The Best Orbit Intelligence Alternatives in 2026 (Questel Compared Honestly)

Hayat Amin CEO of Beyond Elevation · IP strategy & licensing
Hayat Amin 2026-07-21

Orbit Intelligence is Questel's patent search and analytics platform, best known for FamPat, its curated family database. It is a professional tool professionals like. It is also another five-figure line item that many teams renew out of habit rather than need.

Why Teams Leave Orbit

The interface shows its age against newer platforms. Pricing is opaque and negotiated, which means new customers routinely overpay. And like every database in this category, it reports what exists without telling you what to do about it.

The 6 Best Orbit Alternatives in 2026

1. Derwent Innovation — if curation quality is the priority

The other professional-grade curated index. Usually pricier. Compare both quotes and make them bid against each other; see our Derwent alternatives guide for the mirror view.

2. PatSnap — if usability is the priority

Cleaner dashboards, faster onboarding, broader innovation data. Curation is lighter than FamPat. Our PatSnap alternatives guide covers where it wins and loses.

3. PatBase — the price-performance pick for searchers

Comparable family-based searching, typically leaner pricing. The usual destination for search professionals leaving Orbit on cost.

4. Lens.org — free coverage for most day-to-day questions

Prior art, family lookups, competitor monitoring: free. You lose FamPat's curation and some legal-status reliability, which matters for formal FTO work only.

5. Espacenet and USPTO Patent Public Search — free and official

Direct from the patent offices, always current, zero cost. Clunky interfaces, but authoritative for legal-status checks.

6. Bought-in analysis instead of a licence

If Orbit's output lands in one board slide per quarter, you are paying a platform subscription for a report. Commissioning the report directly from a patent strategy consultant is usually cheaper and comes with recommendations attached.

How to Choose

Professional searchers running FTO and invalidity work: stay in the Orbit, Derwent, PatBase tier and negotiate. Everyone else: free databases for lookups, and spend the budget on someone who can turn patent data into filing, licensing, and valuation decisions.

FAQ

How much does Orbit Intelligence cost?

Questel does not publish pricing. Reported contracts run to five figures annually per seat depending on modules, with discounts common at renewal when customers push.

Orbit vs Derwent: which is better?

Both are professional-grade. Derwent's human-curated abstracts edge ahead for searching nuance; Orbit's FamPat families and pricing flexibility win for many corporate users. At this tier, service and price matter more than feature gaps.

Is there a free Orbit alternative?

Lens.org for analytics and coverage, Espacenet for official family and legal-status data. Together they cover most non-specialist needs at zero cost.
