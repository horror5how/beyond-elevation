A founder I sat with last quarter walked away from a $14M acquisition because of a single sentence.

The sentence was in his cofounder agreement from year one. It said the cofounder retained a non-exclusive license to the underlying IP for any future venture in an adjacent market.

Adjacent. That word killed the deal. The acquirer could not get clean rights, and the founder had to either restructure or accept a 40 percent haircut.

This is the cleanup question most founders skip.

Take an hour this weekend. Pull every IP-adjacent document you have ever signed. Cofounder agreements. Vendor contracts. Old advisor agreements. Any AI tool terms of service. Look for the words "license," "retain," "non-exclusive," and "training data."

If you find one of those words and you are not sure what it gives away, send it to me. I will tell you whether it is a five-minute fix or a five-month problem.
