3 in 4 AI founders are using the wrong IP vehicle.

A patent exposes your model architecture to every competitor the day it publishes.

A trade secret keeps it locked forever — if you document it right.

The 4-question test:
1. Can competitors reverse-engineer it from the output? (No = trade secret)
2. Does blocking others require a court injunction? (No = patent)
3. Is the value in the training process, not the claims? (Yes = trade secret)
4. Will it still be commercially relevant in 10 years? (No = trade secret)

Most AI moats are trade secrets pretending to be patents.
