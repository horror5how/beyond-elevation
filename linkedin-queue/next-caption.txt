Your trade secrets are worth more than your patents. Most founders can't prove it.

Acquirers always run three valuations — and take the lowest number.

Cost approach: what it cost to develop × what it costs a competitor to replicate. Sets the floor.

Income approach: the royalty stream a licensee would pay. Real rate: 3–8% of product revenue.

Market approach: comparable trade secret sales from M&A comps and IP exchanges.

If you can't document all three before an LOI lands, your acquirer does it for you.

And they will not be generous.
