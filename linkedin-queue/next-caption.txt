The average founder leaves $2M on the table at exit.

Not bad negotiation. Unactivated IP.

Here is what acquirers find in most data rooms:

Patents never licensed.

Data assets with no contract attached.

Trade secrets with no formal protection program.

Each one is a deduction from your exit multiple.

Three moves that close the gap before the LOI:

Audit every IP asset and assign a dollar value.

Execute one active licensing agreement before diligence.

Put IP on your balance sheet — not as a footnote.

Companies that activate IP before M&A close at 2.4x higher multiples.

That gap is preparation, not luck.
