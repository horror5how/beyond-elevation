Acquirers discount your patent royalties at 3%. They discount your SaaS at 12%.

That 9-point gap is worth $14M on a $5M revenue company.

Here is what the DCF actually shows:

$1M in patent royalties capitalized at 3% = $33M

$1M in SaaS ARR capitalized at 12% = $8M

Same revenue. Four times the exit value.

Royalties are contractual. Senior to churn. Backed by IP that does not expire.

Acquirers price them like bonds. Not subscriptions.

3 moves to capture it:

1. Separate licensing from product on your P&L now.
2. Sign one paying license before your next term sheet.
3. Show the WACC gap in your data room model.
