IP licensing runs at 90%+ gross margin.

No inventory. No COGS. No headcount to scale.

Qualcomm earns 65% of its operating profit from licensing. Not from chips.

The math: your competitor builds the same feature for $4M in R&D. You charge them 4% of net revenue. Forever.

That is not a legal strategy. That is a product.

Founders who figure this out stop treating IP as cost and start running it as their highest-margin business unit.

The ones who don't keep giving their moat away.
