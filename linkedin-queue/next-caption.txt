Most founders sell their IP at exit. The smart ones rent it.

When a buyer acquires your company, you don't have to sell the patent portfolio outright.

Structure it as a licensing earn-out: retain IP ownership, license it to the acquirer.

A lower headline price — but a 4–8% royalty on the acquirer's revenue. Ongoing.

$10M acquisition + 5% royalty on $50M acquirer revenue = $12.5M in post-close income.

Total consideration: $22.5M vs. $10M outright sale.

The IP you spent 5 years building keeps paying after the deal closes.

You need to structure this before the LOI. Not after.
