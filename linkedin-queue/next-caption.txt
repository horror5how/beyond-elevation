The $10M term sheet assigns them 20 years of your IP income.

Three clauses prevent that.

Scope clause: limits assignment to work done with company resources. Pre-existing IP stays yours.

Prosecution clause: you keep rights to file patent continuations. New claims, your royalty stream.

Carve-out clause: pre-existing methods stay licensable to third parties. That income survives the deal.

Most founders never ask for them.

Those who negotiate all three retain an average $2.4M in IP value at Series A.

Read every clause before you sign.
