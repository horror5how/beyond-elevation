73% of AI patents are rejected because founders write the claim wrong.

Not the technology. The words.

USPTO §101: "using AI to achieve outcome X" dies before examination starts.

Your claim must name a specific improvement to a computer system — not a result.

3 elements that turn a rejected AI patent into a licensable asset:
1. Name the technical mechanism, not the output
2. Build dependent claims from broad to narrow
3. Differentiate from prior solutions in the specification

Wrong claim structure costs $280K on average to fix in litigation.
