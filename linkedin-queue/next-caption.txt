Most founders don't know this weapon exists.

When a licensee runs to a cheap court for lower royalty rates, SEP holders have one counter.

An anti-suit injunction.

Huawei used it against InterDigital. Ericsson used it against Apple.

File in a strong jurisdiction. That court orders the licensee to halt the cheap foreign proceedings.

Non-compliance means market access cut off.

The leverage is not the patent. It is where you filed.

Qualcomm earns $6.2B a year in licensing by controlling where disputes are adjudicated.

UK courts now set global FRAND rates at a licensee's request. File in one market and you have already lost.

Jurisdiction is strategy.
