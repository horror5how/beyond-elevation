You can sell your patent once. Or license it 200 times.

Most founders treat IP like real estate you flip.

Licensing is the rental portfolio version.

One patent. 50 licensees. $200K each. That's $10M in royalties — you still own the asset.

Your acquirer pays again at exit.

3 reasons founders skip this:

They think deals take years. (Most close in 60 days.)

They think it needs BigLaw. (A licensing advisor costs less than one BigLaw hour.)

They think their IP isn't valuable. (73% of audited portfolios have a licensable claim.)

Retained ownership + recurring royalties = the capital efficiency play most founders never touch.
