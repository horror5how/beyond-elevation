There is a formula for pricing a data license.

Almost nobody uses it.

Founders price data like a product: cost to collect, plus margin. Buyers ignore that number.

Buyers care about their alternative cost — what it would cost them to replicate the dataset themselves.

The formula: License Value = Buyer's Alternative Cost × 25–40%

A dataset that cost $80k to build can command $400k per year from the right buyer.

The formula does not change the data. It changes who controls the negotiation.
