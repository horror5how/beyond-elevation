68% of tech acquisitions surface an IP chain-of-title gap at due diligence.

The average valuation hit: 30%.

It almost always comes from 3 sources.

A contractor wrote core code. No IP assignment clause.

A co-founder built the first prototype. Left before the SHA. No IP deed.

An employee filed the founding invention. Left. No assignment back.

The acquirer's IP counsel traces every asset to its creator. If the chain breaks, the deal reprices.

You have 14 days to resolve a title issue cleanly before it becomes a permanent haircut.

Most founders find out when the LOI arrives.

Run the audit before they do.
