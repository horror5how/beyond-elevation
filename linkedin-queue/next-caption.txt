Qualcomm collected $7.7B in licensing fees in 2023. During COVID, licensing dropped less than 2%. Their product revenue dropped 18%.

That spread is structural. Not luck.

IP royalties have a contractual floor that product ARR never will.

Minimum guarantee clauses. 20-year patent terms. Licensees pay even when they're cutting headcount.

ARM's licensing income grew during the 2020 chip shortage.

InterDigital collected royalties through the 2009 crisis without a single termination.

Most founders build revenue that tracks the economy.

Smart ones build a second line that tracks the contract.
