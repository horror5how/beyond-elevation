# LinkedIn Post Template (P→M→R)

STRUCTURE (enforced):
1. HOOK (line 1) — ≤9 words, contrarian or numbered, ends with period or colon. Stops scroll.
2. PAIN (1–2 lines) — the specific cost of NOT solving this, named in CEO terms.
3. MECHANISM (3–5 numbered steps OR bullets) — the actual "how", concrete moves.
4. RESULT (1 line) — proof: a specific number, a client outcome, or a stat.
5. CTA (1 line) — a binary or open question that invites a comment ("↳ Founders: which X would hurt most?").

AUDIENCE: founders / CEOs / Seed–Series B operators. NOT: IP lawyers, paralegals, legal practitioners.
FORBIDDEN OPENERS: "In today's...", "Companies are...", "It's no secret that...", abstract noun phrases.
FORBIDDEN: em-dashes, "leverage" (as verb), "delve", "seamless", "navigate the landscape".

EXAMPLE (use as model):
> 87% of CEOs just signed an AI contract that owns their best IP.
>
> Here's the trap: your team uses Cursor/Copilot. Output gets sucked into the vendor's training set. You don't own the moat — they do.
>
> The fix (3 moves, this week):
> 1. Audit every AI tool for "training data" clauses
> 2. Switch to enterprise tiers with no-train guarantees
> 3. Register IP on AI-generated work BEFORE shipping
>
> One BE client found $14M of unprotected IP in 11 days. Same playbook in comments.
>
> ↳ Founders: which AI tool would hurt most if its vendor owned your output?
